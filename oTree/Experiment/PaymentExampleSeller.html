Example 1:
The lottery pays exactly 50 coins with a probability of 40% and nothing otherwise. You choose to offer this lottery for 25 coins. The buyer states a maximum willingness to pay of 20 coins. Therefore, you are not able to sell the lottery and cannot earn an additional bonus.

Example 2:
The lottery pays exactly 0 coins with a probability of 20%, exactly 60 coins with 30% probability and exactly 70 coins with 50% probability. You choose a selling price of 40 coins. The buyer states a maximum willingness to pay of 45 coins. You sell the lottery for the selling price of 40 coins and can earn a bonus of 40 coins.
