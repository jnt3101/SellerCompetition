Your Role

You have been assigned the role of a buyer. In this part of the study, you will be shown lottery offers made by other participants. Your task is to evaluate these lotteries, state your beliefs about them, and decide whether you want to buy one of them.


Your Decisions
{% if group.group_type == "1S1B" %}

In your group, there is one seller and one buyer. You will therefore see one lottery that is offered by another participant in the study.

For this lottery, we will first ask you how often you think you would win the highest prize if you played this lottery 100 times. This is simply your personal belief about how frequently the highest payoff would occur.

After stating your belief, you will be asked to decide whether you want to buy this lottery at the price that the seller has chosen. You can either buy the lottery or choose not to buy it.

{% else %}

In your group, there are multiple sellers. This means you will see more than one lottery, each offered by a different participant in the study.

For each of the lotteries, we will first ask you how often you think you would win the highest prize if you played the lottery 100 times. These statements are simply your personal beliefs.

After stating your beliefs, you will have to decide whether you want to buy a lottery, and if so, which one. You may purchase at most one lottery. This means you may choose to:

  • Buy the lottery offered by seller 1,
  • Buy the lottery offered by seller 2,
  • Or choose not to buy any lottery.
{% endif %}
Your Bonus Payment

Your bonus payment will depend on your decision. If you buy a lottery, the computer will draw the outcome of that lottery and this outcome determines your bonus.

If you decide not to buy any lottery, your bonus will instead be equal to the lowest price among all lotteries offered in your group.

All coins earned in the study will be converted into British pounds at the rate of {{ exchange_rate }} coins = 1 British pound.


Important

Please make sure to answer all questions carefully and according to your true beliefs and preferences. Your decisions, as well as the decisions of the other participants in your group, will directly influence your potential bonus payment at the end of the study.
