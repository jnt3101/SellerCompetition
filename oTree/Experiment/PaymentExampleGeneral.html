Example 1:
The lottery pays exactly 50 coins with a probability of 40% and nothing otherwise. The seller chose a price of 15 coins for the lottery. The buyer does not want to purchase the lottery for 15 coins. Therefore, the seller does not receive a bonus payment and the bonus payment for the buyer is 15 coins.

Example 2:
The lottery pays exactly 0 coins with a probability of 20%, exactly 60 coins with 30% probability and exactly 70 coins with 50% probability. The price is set at 40 coins and the buyer decides to purchase the lottery. The random draw of the lottery generates an outcome of 60 coins. Therefore, the sellers' bonus is 40 coins and the buyers' bonus is 60 coins
