Your Role

You have been assigned the role of a seller. In this part of the study, you will create an offer for a lottery that will be shown to the buyer(s) in your group. Your lottery may or may not be purchased, depending on the decisions of the buyer(s).


Your Decisions

Each seller in the study offers a lottery to the buyer(s). Before setting your price, you will be shown information about the lottery you are selling, including a set of sample draws from it.

We will then ask you to make several decisions:

  • Choose how to present the lottery from several available presentation formats.
  • State your price for the lottery. This is the amount at which you offer the lottery to the buyer(s).
  • State a second-order belief: that is, how often you think the buyer(s) believe that the highest prize of the lottery would be won if the lottery were played 100 times. In other words, you indicate what you think the buyers’ belief is about the frequency of the jackpot.

Market Environment
{% if group.group_type == "1S1B" %}

In your group, there is one seller and one buyer. The buyer only sees your lottery offer. The buyer will state their belief about the lottery and decide whether to buy it at your chosen price.

{% elif group.group_type == "2S1B" %}

In your group, there are two sellers and one buyer. This means the buyer will be shown two different lotteries: yours and the one offered by the other seller.

The buyer can purchase at most one lottery. They may choose:

  • Your lottery,
  • The lottery of the other seller,
  • Or to not buy any lottery.

Your lottery therefore competes with the lottery offered by the other seller.

{% else %}

In your group, there are two sellers and two buyers. Each buyer will see both sellers’ lotteries.

Each buyer can buy at most one lottery, but both buyers may buy the same lottery. There is no competition between buyers.

This means:

  • Both buyers may buy your lottery,
  • Both may buy the other seller’s lottery,
  • Each may choose a different seller,
  • Or either buyer may decide not to buy at all.

As a seller, you therefore compete with the other seller for the purchase decisions of two independent buyers.

{% endif %}
Your Bonus Payment

Your bonus depends on whether your lottery is purchased.

  • If at least one buyer buys your lottery, your bonus equals the price that you set for the lottery.
  • If no buyer buys your lottery, your bonus is 0.

All coins earned in the study will be converted into British pounds at the rate of {{ exchange_rate }} coins = 1 British pound.


Important

Please make all decisions carefully. Your bonus depends on your pricing decision, your chosen presentation of the lottery, and on how attractive the buyers perceive your lottery to be—both in terms of their own beliefs and in comparison to competing lotteries in your group.
